Famous Coffees

Cappuccino

The **cappuccino** originated in Italy and derives its name from the Capuchin monks, whose robes resembled the drink’s color. Cappuccino became popular in the 20th century as espresso machines improved, allowing baristas to perfect the balance of espresso, steamed milk, and foam. It started gaining international recognition after World War II, particularly in Europe and North America, becoming a café staple.

Americano

The **Americano** originated during World War II when American soldiers in Italy found espresso too strong. To make it more like the coffee they were used to back home, they diluted espresso with hot water. The name "Americano" comes from this American adaptation. Over time, it became a popular way to enjoy a milder coffee, both in Europe and the United States.

Latte

The **latte** originated in Italy, where "caffè latte" simply means "milk coffee." It traditionally consists of espresso mixed with steamed milk, with little to no foam. While Italians typically drink it at breakfast, the modern café latte, with its creamy texture and latte art, gained popularity in the 20th century, especially in the U.S. and other parts of the world, becoming a favorite for those who prefer a less intense coffee flavor.
